Identifying the problem with cinemas

Imagine yourself as a star trek fan trying to see a new blockbuster movie at the theater, only to encounter the annoyingly long ticket lines, then finally receiving your tickets to discover that your preferred seat in the theater is already taken or that you were unable to reschedule your ticket booking.

This frequently occurs at movie theatres, which got us to thinking about how we can make it simple for people to find and book movie tickets to prevent stress and delays during peak movie-going hours.

Introducing Reel

Reel is a cinema app that helps users locate nearby cinemas in their area, and make ticket bookings, snack purchases, and seat reservations all in one place to save users the stress and delays involved in making physical bookings at cinemas. The app's main aim is to provide a stressless cinema booking experience.

It all began as a personal experience that I gradually became aware of, and when I ran into a startup with the same business idea, I was eager to take on the challenge.

Gaining perspective on the problem

Having identified the project goal, I needed to understand the real-time issues surrounding ticket bookings and seat reservations in cinemas. I spoke to a couple of individuals at a cinema asking about their feelings and thoughts around ticket, snacks and seat booking in exchange for a drink for their time. Then i conducted an online survey with 20 individuals who like to visit cinemas frequently with the aim of gaining insight into the experience and challenges of making cinema reservations.

Identifying insights

Speaking to the people i met at the cinema and combining the surey data revealed that 75% of the participants had experienced difficulties and delays in the process of making cinema reservations, the major pain points they stated included

01

Navigation of existing systems

The participants find it difficult to navigate cinema websites and make cinema reservations

02

Crowdy situations and long queues

Some individuals avoid the cinema due to crowdy situations and long queues

“I found it difficult to choose my seat because I couldn't comprehend the cinema area (distance between scene and seat). I always select the center position. I will put off going to the movies if no center seats are available.”

User Interview participant

03

Making seat reservation accessible

The research showed there’s a need to make the seat reservation process accessible and simplified due to the inaccessibility of existing reservation processes.

04

Payment services flexibility

Another identified need is for flexibility in the payment method and trustworthiness of the system to ensure secure payment for ticket booking and other required services.

Analyzing existing products

From the user research phase, some of the participants identified they had experienced difficulties in using cinema websites, I also needed to analyze the market for cinema booking, understanding the structure, and gaps in the market. I conducted an in-depth analysis on 3 products in the market comparing the experience and functionalities across the products.

Key Findings

01

Poor utilization of location services

Location services are not very prevalent in existing cinema booking apps, there is a need for easy access to information on cinemas based on proximity to the user.

02

Seat reservation system

Most of the websites especially in Nigeria do not have a proper system for seat reservations and snacks purchase online.

UX Strategy

Identifying and understanding the users’ needs

Based on the data gotten from the research phase I identified patterns in the participants’ responses and preferences, this showed there was a similarity in the users’ goals and frustrations, so I created a persona to give a better understanding and serve as a reference point for individuals the problem is being solved for

Defining the problem

Based on the user persona, I was able to identify the problem statement referencing the persona to give a clear description of the user’s needs.

“David is a busy freelancer who needs an easy and secure way to make ticket bookings and seat reservations online because it would help avoid delays and stress at the cinema”

Journey Mapping

After defining the issue clearly and creating a persona, I looked for opportunities for improvement by identifying the typical experiences people have when going to the movies, buying tickets, and getting snacks.

Creating an efficient flow

Identifying improvement opportunities with the user journey revealed clear flows to help users achieve their goals, here I clearly visualized the path to be taken by users in completing their primary task on the app. I thought of the simplest ways users can easily carry out their tasks without getting lost or confused in the process while making use of the user story.

Designing the experience.

Sketches x Digital wireframes

I drew multiple versions of each screen and iterated on them to get a clearer structure for each proposed page after gaining an understanding of the user flow and proposed solution.

Experience 1

Identifying the usability of the product was critical early in the design process, so I created digital wireframe prototype to visualize the user flow and various use cases of the product.

Testing, testing.

Have we really made cinema booking a seamless process?

Right after creating early-stage prototypes, I conducted the first phase of the usability test to identify areas that could be a problem in the user flow. This was done through unmoderated studies with 5 participants in which they carried out tasks on the prototype.

Key Findings

01

3 out of 5 participants had difficulties finding the location setting.

03

3 out of the 5 participants had issues clicking on certain icons from the homepage showing click-ability issues concerning the rule of thumb.

02

4 out of 5 participants had difficulties using the back/return buttons and returning to the homepage showing faults in the navigation process.

Visual Design Guide

I used two typefaces for the user interface to keep things simple and easy to read. I also chose red as the primary color of the app because of its vibrancy, as well as to provide users with a sense of familiarity due to its association with commonly used red cinema chairs.

Second round of testing

Using the insights from the first round of testing to modify the wireframes, I made high-fidelity designs for further usability testing. I also conducted remote unmoderated testing with 5 participants to identify areas of friction in the user experience.

Key Findings

01

Some of the icons were too small for easy interaction showing issues relating to the rule of thumb

02

The seat arrangements were not well labeled for easy decision making

Presenting the refined cinema booking experience

Onboarding the user

This section of the app is to inform the users of the important features and advantages the app has to offer, then collect the user’s account information to keep specific booking data. I designed these to stimulate the user’s interest.

The booking experience

There are 2 user paths- two ways the user can complete the task of ticket booking, snacks purchase, and seat reservation.

Through this path, the user can select a preferred movie check the details, select a date and a nearby cinema to watch it at. The user would be able to view the movie ratings as well as other movie details before selecting a cinema to visit.

Here the user could select a cinema and view their details before choosing a movie. I added a distance estimator as well as a cinema review tab to help users in deciding which cinema to visit.

Checking Out

The checkout process is designed to provide the users with a smooth experience. I incorporated multiple payment options to ensure payment flexibility.

Rescheduling and User profile

These include the purchased tickets, favorites, and profile page. I included a feature to allow users to reschedule their movie tickets and also access customer support.

Design impact.

Measured based on initial user feedbacks.

The design of our cinema app prioritizes ease of use and accessibility for all users. The clean and modern interface allows for quick and seamless navigation to find and purchase tickets and make seat reservations for upcoming films.

To ensure that the app is accessible to all users, we have included high-contrast color schemes and larger text options for users with visual impairments. We also included voice commands to facilitate navigation for users with mobility impairments.

User testing and feedback have been incredibly positive, with a significant increase in app engagement and ticket purchases. Our app's design has also received praise for its ability to cater to diverse user needs and preferences.

Reflection

This project helped me learn more about the importance of research to the design process as it guides every design decision made. I did a lot of iteration in the process and it also helped me realize the importance of user testing at every step.

Moving forward, I would like to incorporate more accessibility features to ensure that the app is useful for a wider range of users. Also, I’d like to conduct more usability testing to identify any area of difficulty and to see if the user’s needs are met.
